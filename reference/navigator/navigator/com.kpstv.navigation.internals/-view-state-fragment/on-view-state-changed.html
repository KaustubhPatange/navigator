open fun onViewStateChanged(viewState: ViewStateFragment.ViewState)

Notifies when the View associated with this Fragment goes to ViewState.BACKGROUND or comes to ViewState.FOREGROUND.

When you add a new Fragment to the container through FragmentManager add transaction, chances are the previous Fragment's view will still be present in the container, in such case onPause, onStop, etc. are not called for that fragment.

This callback is associated with the View& not to the Fragment's lifecycle.

  • ViewState.FOREGROUND means the view is created & is visible to the user. Called after onViewCreated.

  • ViewState.BACKGROUND means the view is not the current visible view in the container. This doesn't guarantee that onDestroyView will always be called after this state change.
